I'm a mid-30s dev who spent six years at a FAANG company watching simple deploys turn into 47-service Rube Goldberg machines. I quit to build indie products on my own terms.

Now I run profitable apps on a handful of cheap Hetzner boxes. No Kubernetes. No serverless. No $10K/mo cloud bills. Just Docker Compose, a Makefile, and a healthy distrust of anything that requires a "platform team."

**Exit Cloud** is where I write about the tools and workflows that actually work at small scale — self-hosted infrastructure, AI-assisted dev, and modern deployment for indie devs and small teams. Every tutorial is reproducible on a $5-20/mo VPS. No theory, no marketing speak, just real configs and real commands.

### What I run

- A couple of Hetzner VPSes (~$40/mo total)
- Docker Compose for everything
- Caddy for reverse proxy + auto-TLS
- Kamal for zero-downtime deploys
- PostgreSQL + Redis
- BorgBackup + Backblaze B2
- Plausible for privacy-friendly analytics
- Uptime Kuma for monitoring
- Tailscale for secure access

### What I believe

- You don't need Kubernetes. You need a VPS and a Makefile.
- The best monitoring is a cron job that curls your health endpoint.
- If your deploy takes more than 60 seconds, something is wrong.
- AI coding tools are force multipliers, not replacements.
- Self-hosting isn't about saving money — it's about owning your stack.
