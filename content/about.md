I'm a mid-30s dev who spent six years at a FAANG company watching simple deploys turn into 47-service Rube Goldberg machines. I quit to build indie products on my own terms.

Now I run profitable apps on a handful of cheap [Hetzner](https://www.hetzner.com/) boxes. No Kubernetes. No serverless. No $10K/mo cloud bills. Just [Docker Compose](https://docs.docker.com/compose/), a Makefile, and a healthy distrust of anything that requires a "platform team."

**Exit Cloud** is where I write about the tools and workflows that actually work at small scale — self-hosted infrastructure, AI-assisted dev, and modern deployment for indie devs and small teams. Every tutorial is reproducible on a $5-20/mo VPS. No theory, no marketing speak, just real configs and real commands.

### What I run

- A couple of [Hetzner](https://www.hetzner.com/) VPSes (~$40/mo total)
- [Docker Compose](https://docs.docker.com/compose/) for everything
- [Caddy](https://caddyserver.com/) for reverse proxy + auto-TLS
- [Kamal](https://kamal-deploy.org/) for zero-downtime deploys
- [PostgreSQL](https://www.postgresql.org/) + [Redis](https://redis.io/)
- [BorgBackup](https://www.borgbackup.org/) + [Backblaze B2](https://www.backblaze.com/cloud-storage)
- [Plausible](https://plausible.io/) for privacy-friendly analytics
- [Uptime Kuma](https://github.com/louislam/uptime-kuma) for monitoring
- [Tailscale](https://tailscale.com/) for secure access

### What I believe

- You don't need Kubernetes. You need a VPS and a Makefile.
- The best monitoring is a cron job that curls your health endpoint.
- If your deploy takes more than 60 seconds, something is wrong.
- AI coding tools are force multipliers, not replacements.
- Self-hosting isn't about saving money — it's about owning your stack.
